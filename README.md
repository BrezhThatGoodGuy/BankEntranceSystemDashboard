"AUTOMATED BANK ENTRANCE SECURITY SYSTEM"
==============================================


Bank entrance security systems, commonly known as man-trap or booth controllers, 
are widely used in Zimbabwe and other regions to regulate access into financial
institutions. While these systems provide basic security, they remain limited by
heavy reliance on human intervention, lack of intelligent decision-making, and
minimal support for user guidance or predictive maintenance. As a result, banks
remain vulnerable to robberies, operational inefficiencies, and system downtime.



This project proposes the design and implementation of an Automated BankEntrance
Control System enhanced with artificial intelligence integration. The
system will utilize AI-based image recognition to automatically detect potential
threats such as masked faces, rapid entry attempts, or weapons, enabling real-time
denial of entry. Automated operation mode switching will replace manual keyswitches, 
improving emergency response and reducing hardware wear.
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Navigation assistance through voice or display prompts will guide customers, particularly the
elderly, to reduce congestion and misuse in the entrance booths. Additionally,
predictive maintenance will be supported by real-time error reporting from sensors,
enabling early detection of component malfunctions and minimizing downtime.



The proposed solution integrates a PIC16F877A microcontroller with AI decision
inputs, door control hardware, and a mobile/web dashboard for remote monitoring.
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------


The expected outcome is a cost-effective, reliable, and user-friendly prototype that
not only enhances security but also improves customer experience and system
sustainability. This innovation has the potential for broader applications in other
high-security environments such as data centers, laboratories, and cash-handling
facilities.
